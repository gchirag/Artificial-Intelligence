<H1>
Thierse speech regarding National Mourning Day
</H1>
President of the Bundestag (parliament), Mr. Thierse, dismissed calls for
putting an end to German history. "Leaving behind history and denying
past horrors are impossible," Thierse said during a commemoration of
National Mourning Day at the Capitol in Berlin. He added that looking
onto the past does not evoke a gloomy image exclusively. "Next to
mourning and many painful memories there are also hope and faith,"
Thierse stressed.

<H1>
Chancellor Schroeder challenges party to be united
</H1>
Three weeks before the national social democratic convention in Berlin
party chief Schroeder asked for unity among party ranks. At a meeting of
East German State delegates in Berlin the chancellor said that people
want a strong and unified social democratic party.
Kicking off the conference Brandenburg Governor Stolpe warned that the
topic of social justice must not be neglected. The Social Democrats must
remain clearly recognizable as the party of the common man.

<H1>
Klimmt demands consideration for Green Party's concerns
</H1>
Minister of Transportation, Mr. Klimmt, challenged the Social Democrats
to pay more attention to the Green Party. According to the Berlin Daily
News Klimmt maintained that the SPD stands for social justice, reducing
unemployment and creating new jobs, while the Green Party is more
concerned with abandoning nuclear energy and focusing on alternative
transportation. He continued to say there is a need to be considerate,
even for the bigger partner in a coalition.

<H1>
Divided opinions about Otto Schily's asylum proposals
</H1>
Minister of the Interior Otto Schily's call for the reform of immigration
law is leading to squabbles in the coalition. Green Party Chief Ms.
Mueller said there would no tightening of the law under her party's
reign. On ZDF, the German Public Broadcast, Ms. Mueller referred to the
coalition treaty and added that the basic right of asylum must not
degenerate into an act of mercy. A similar position was voiced by the
Commissioner of Foreigners, Beck, also a member of the Green Party.
In contrast, Hesse Governor Koch offered Mr. Schily the Christian
Democrats' support if he were to present his ideas to reform immigration
law in a comprehensive bill.

<H1>
Politicians in favor of pardon for former members of the Politburo
</H1>
Politicians of all parties are in favor of pardoning the former members
of the East German Politburo, Mr. Krenz, Mr. Schabowski and Mr. Kleiber.
The former civil rights activist, Ms. Lengsfeld, was quoted in "Welt am
Sonntag," "This will keep the defendants from being viewed as martyrs.
Christian Democrat member of the parliament, Mr. Eppelmann, said that
before being pardoned these former East German politicians would have to
admit their fault. Mr. Krenz did not strike him with such an impression.

<H1>
Free Democrats demand resignation of Gerhard in Brandenburg
</H1>
The Free Democrats in Brandenburg asked party chief Mr. Gerhardt to
resign. During a special party convention in Potsdam a clear majority of
delegates approved a request for a new beginning in terms of personnel
and policy. The document states that Mr. Gerhard does not implement the
party line effectively. His resignation would be a convincing and much
expected gesture of looking toward the future.

<H1>
Soccer: Otto Rehagel extends contract with Kaiserslautern
</H1>
Otto Rehagel has extended his contract as coach of 1.FC Kaiserslautern
until 2002. During the last few weeks rumors had it that Rehagel would
quit.
