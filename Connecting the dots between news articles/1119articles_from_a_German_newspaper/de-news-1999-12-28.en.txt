<H1>
Snow and ice hamper clean-up after hurricane Lothar
</H1>
Snow and icy roads obstructed traffic in most parts of Baden
Wuerttemberg. Up to 40 cm of fresh snow fell in the Black Forest. This
also hampers the clean-up after hurricane Lothar.

<H1>
Possibly investigations against Kohl because of donations affaire
</H1>
An investigation against former CDU president Helmut Kohl because
of not declared party donations is coming closer. This evening, the
prosecution has not yet decided whether to start investigations. A
spokesperson for the prosecution said that any [circulating]
information about imminent investigations did not originate from the
prosecutor's office.

<H1>
Nuclear fuel containers need new coating
</H1>
Nuclear fuel casks (CASTORs) in the intermediary storage
facility in Ahaus are rusting. Now, they must be sanded and get a new
coat of paint. The economics ministry of North Rhine-Westphalia stated
that there was no danger for the painters.

<H1>
Extra pay for work on Sundays to remain tax-free
</H1>
The extra pay for work at night, on Sundays, and on public
holidays is to remain tax free. Spokespersons of SPD and
Buendnis90/Gruene said the government parties had always rejected the
taxation of this extra pay.

<H1>
Gasoline and diesel prices increase significantly
</H1>
The prices per litre for gasoline and diesel are now just
under 2 DM. Gas increased by 0.04 DM, diesel by 0.05 DM. In addition,
there will be a 0.07 DM eco-tax as of January 1, 2000.

<H1>
Police apprehend two teenage burglar gangs
</H1>
Police have apprehended two gangs of burglars. One group had
seven members and was headed by a 12-year-old. The other gang consisted
of five boys aged between 14 and 17 years. The teenagers allegedly
burglarized apartments and shops in series.

<H1>
Police search for escaped prisoner in Brandenburg
</H1>
An escaped prisoner is searched in Brandenburg. A 29-year-old
murderer did not return to the Brandenburg-Havel prison after his
prison holiday. The man is considered dangerous.
