<H1>
Serbs want to suspend repatriation of Kosovo-Albanians
</H1>
The Serb Government provisionally wants to suspend the repatriation
agreement of Kosovo-Albanians with Germany. In Belgrade, the EU-wide
landing restriction against the national Yugoslav air line was mentioned
as the reason. In any case, the landing restriction makes all deportations
to Yugoslavia impossible.

<H1>
Poland declines demands of exiled
</H1>
Poland declined the renewed demand of the exiled's association for
compensation. Over radio Warsaw, the Minister of Foreign Affairs,
Geremec, said that this kind of payments will have to be made by the Federal
Government in Bonn. For Poland, this affair is closed for all times. The
exiled's association denied the maturity of Poland and the Czech Republic
to join the EU. At the same time it had renewed its demand that both
countries should only be allowed to join the EU after having granted the
exiled the right to return and having paid compensation.

<H1>
Hand grenade exploded in Bundeswehr barracks
</H1>
In the Bundeswehr barracks in Amberg, Upper Palatinate (Oberpfalz), 21
soldiers were injured in an explosion of a splinter grenade. According to
the Ministry of Defence in Bonn, one soldier's life is still in danger. The
grenade detonated during an exercise in a training room. A speaker of the
Ministry of Defence mentioned improper handling of the explosive as the
reason.

<H1>
Daimler blackmailer on trial
</H1>
A 46-year-old man goes on trial before a court on the charge of having
tried to blackmail the Daimler-Benz trust. He threatened publicly to kill
one Mercedes driver every week, if he did not receive DM five million.

<H1>
Negative reaction on charge for consulting specialists
</H1>
A huge majority declined the proposal of a DM 100.00 charge for consulting
specialists without a medical prescription. Panel-doctors, health insurance
companies and labour unions firmly rejected this proposal. The federal
association of local health insurance companies called it a penalty tax for
insured people. The charge was demanded by the chairman of the doctors'
association of Schleswig-Holstein, Volkmann. He wanted to prevent expensive
special diagnostics of specialists from being used too often.

<H1>
Soccer: Vogts resigns as national coach
</H1>
Berti Vogts is the first national coach in German soccer history, to
resigns prematurely. Facing the extraordinary pressure from the public, he
saw no possibility anymore to train the national team with the required
strength in order to qualify for the European Championship. Vogts'
predecessor, Franz Beckenbauer, proposes the ex-coach of Real Madrid, Jupp
Heynckes, as new national coach.

<H1>
Tennis: Graf eliminated from US Open, Gross in next round
</H1>
In New York, Steffi Graf was eliminated from the US Open. In the
eighth-finals, she lost to the Swiss player Schnyder (3:6 and 4:6). Oliver
Gross is the only German, who has reached the eighth-finals in the men's
competition. He won in five sets against player Jeff Grant from the United
States.
