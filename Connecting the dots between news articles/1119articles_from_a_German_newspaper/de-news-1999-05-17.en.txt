<H1>
Schroeder and Dalema discuss Italian peace plan
</H1>
Federal Chancellor Schroeder met with Italian leader Dalema in Bari.
Main topic was the newest Italian peace plan for the settlement of the
Yugoslavian War.  Dalema's proposal entails a NATO cease-fire, should
Russia and China vote against Belgrade in a UN resolution.  Should
Yugoslav President Milosevic renege on a pullout of his troops from
Kosovo, Dalema would plead for the deployment of NATO ground forces.
Foreign Minister Fischer foresees no majority in the Bundestag for
sending in ground troops.

<H1>
Eichel challenges Ministries to save
</H1>
Finance Minister Eichel challenged the Federal Ministries in writing
to
share in the coverage of financial gaps according to share in the
Federal budget.  Eichel wrote  in a letter to his ministry colleagues
that the rehabilitation of the budget is an economic task for the
whole
federal government.  Each department must contribute.  Only thus can
the
march into debtor status be stopped.  The financial situation of the
Federal Republic is dramatic; a deficit of  30 billion Marks in the
2000
budget, and though 2003 50 billion.  The strictest cutbacks of 13
billion fall upon Employment and Social Minister Riester.  The Defense
Ministry has to cut 3.5 billion Marks.

<H1>
Muentefering refuses speed limit
</H1>
The Federal Environmental Bureau and Traffic Minister Muentefering are
fighting over the speed limit.  The Bureau is for a 100 km/hr. limit
on
highways and 30 km/h within cities, which should bring more travelling
security, and lower noise and pollutants.  Muentefering refuses these
speed limits, saying there's nothing about it in the coalition
contract
with the Greens.

<H1>
Attack on residence of German Ambassador to Athens
</H1>
An attack was made on the residence of the German Ambassador to Greece
last night.  According to Greek police, 2 bazookas were fired at the
building, which was damaged.  Ambassador Kuna and his wife were in the
house at the time of the attack, but were not injured.  Nine days
earlier a bomb exploded near by at the Dutch Ambassador's residence.

<H1>
Deutsche Bank not merging with Italian bank group
</H1>
Deutsche Bank denied a newspaper report about a supposed planned
merger
with the Italian bank group Unicredito.  The rumors were completely
unfounded, said a spokesperson in Frankfurt.  The Duesseldorf
Handelsblatt reported that there were signs that Deutsche Bank would
propose a takeover or stock exchange offer today.

<H1>
French Telecom wants to sue Deutsche Telecom
</H1>
France Telecom wants to sue Deutsche Telecom for damages due to the
planned merger with Telecom Italia.  The previous partner of the
Germans
turned to the International Chamber of Commerce for a judgement.  The
damages amount to several billion Euros.  The French see a breach of
contract, because Deutsche Telecom allegedly made merger plans with
the
Italians without putting it to a vote.

<H1>
Rhine flood subsides
</H1>
The flooding in southern Germany and Switzerland had gone down last
night.  According to Mannheim's River Police, the flood waters are
sinking or stagnating.  The Rhine transport is further resting at
about
280 km between Rheinfelden
and Mannheim.

<H1>
Dillingen massacre presumed to be revenge
</H1>
The fugitive assassin from Dillingen had allegedly acted out of
revenge.  One of the victims occasionally worked for the police as an
informant.  He incriminated accused perpetrator Guenther Ewen last
year

wounded

<H1>
No expansion of air traffic from Duesseldorf airport
</H1>
The Upper Administrative Court of Muenster stopped the expansion of
air
traffic from the Duesseldorf airport.  The judgement followed the
complaints of residents.  According to the judgement, the State
government falsely estimated the effect on the population of the
expansion in business.
