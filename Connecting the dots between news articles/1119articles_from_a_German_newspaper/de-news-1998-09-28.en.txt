<H1>
Preliminary official final result of German General Elections
</H1>
The SPD is the clear winner of the General Elections and its candidate,
the State Premier of Lower Saxony, Gerhard Schroeder, will become the
new Chancellor. According to the preliminary final result announced by
election organisers this morning, the SPD obtained 40.9%. The CDU/CSU
finished second with 35.1%, B90/The Greens reached 6.7% and the FDP
achieved 6.2%. The fifth party in the Parliament is the PDS with 5.1%.
This year, 82.3% of the eligible voters voted.

<H1>
SPD wants to start coalition talks with the Greens
</H1>
According to the future Chancellor Schroeder, the SPD will negotiate
already on Friday with the Greens about government coalition. One day
after his tremendous victory, Schroeder said that he sees no
unbridgeable difficulties. The talks shall begin on Friday. The
designated Chancellor mentioned Just Stollmann as one of the Ministers
and as Minister of Finance, Gerhard Schroeder was talking of SPD-leader
Oskar Lafontaine. By the end of the coalition talks with the Greens it
shall be determined, who will do which job in the new government.
Schroeder regretted that the Union refused coalition talks so early and
reduced the options of the SPD by this.

<H1>
Schroeder against an increase of gasoline tax
</H1>
The future Chancellor Schroeder sees only few possibilities for the
increase of the gasoline tax, demanded by the Greens. On ZDF, Schroeder
said he had defined himself as a "car man". It is very reasonable to
increase the ecological aspect of the tax system, but there must not be
any sole actions in Europe.

<H1>
Good chances for Schaeuble to become chairman CDU
</H1>
For the Union as the loser of the elections the successors are partly
clear. The resigning party leader, Helmut Kohl, did not hide his
thoughts. He would like to see parliamentary party leader Schaeuble as
new chairman. Next week, the CDU will announce proposals for a new team
after this election disaster. After Kohl'S announcement of his
resignation, parliamentary party leader Schaeuble has the best chances
to become also chairman of the CDU. The CDU directorate is still
discussing about a team, consisting of Schaeuble and the actual Minister
of Defence, Ruehe. Once again, Kohl accepts the whole responsibility for
the defeat.

<H1>
Waigel wants to pass the CSU-chairmanship to Stoiber
</H1>
In Munich, also CSU-leader Waigel announced his resignation. Bavaria's
State Premier, Stoiber, had been very surprised. This evening, Stoiber
said to the BR (Bayerischer Rundfunk) that Waigel's decision deserves
acknowledgement and respect. There had been no reason for the CSU-leader
to resign. Nobody of the party had been prepared for this. Stoiber did
not tell any details, if he will be a candidate next year as Waigel's
successor. The CSU-parliamentary group in the Bavarian State Assembly
denominated Stoiber unanimous for a second term as State Premier. On
September 13th, the CSU won the elections and could increase its
absolute majority up to 52.9%.

<H1>
Final Result of the Regional Elections in Mecklenburg West-Pomerania
</H1>
Also in the Regional Elections in Mecklenburg West-Pomerania the SPD
became the strongest party. According to the preliminary final result
the SPD finished with 34.3%, that means 4.8% plus compared to 1994. The
CDU lost 7.5% and finished with 30.2%. The PDS increased its share from
22.7% in 1994 up to 24.4%. All other parties did not make it into the
parliament, because they had less than 5%. The state assembly in
Schwerin looks now like that: SPD - 72 seats CDU - 24 seats PDS - 20
seats The leading candidate of the SPD, Ringstorff, announced
negotiations with the PDS and the CDU about a coalition.

<H1>
56.4% in Schleswig-Holstein against orthography reform
</H1>
In Schleswig-Holstein, in the future the pupils will be taught the old
orthography again. In a referendum, 56.4% of the electors voted against
the enforcement of the orthography reform. Schleswig-Holstein is the
only federal state, where the new orthography is not taught. The
Minister of Cultural Issues, Boerg, regretted this result. Government
leader Simonis announced to talk with her colleagues from the other
states, but she does not expect the other State Premiers to take back
the orthography reform.

<H1>
No German edition of the Lewinsky report
</H1>
There will be no German edition of the Starr-Report, which had been
publicly announced. According to the Muenchner Verlag there are 9000
orders for the book, but the printing and all marketing activities had
been stopped. The company justified the stop with the exaggerated
reporting after the publication of President Clinton's interrogation by
the Grand Jury.
