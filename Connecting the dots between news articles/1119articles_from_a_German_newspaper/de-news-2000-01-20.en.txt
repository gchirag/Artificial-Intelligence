<H1>
Schaeuble apologizes for donations scandal
</H1>
Following daily new revelations, CDU Chairman Schaeuble apologized to the
Bundestag for the donations scandal of his party. He regretted in
particular "that it was in our responsibility that it was obviously
contravened against the law, and that we have damaged trust in the
integrity of democratic parties and institutions".
SPD Chairman Struck calls the scandal the biggest political scandal in
Germany's history.
SPD and The Greens want to expand the task of the investigations committe
to include a clarification of the CDU's complete illegal financial affairs.

<P>
After today's first meeting of the committe, Chairman Volker Naumann (SPD)
announced that his party, The Greens, and also the FDP will submit an
appropriate proposal to the Bundestag in February. The members of the
committee also agreed on a list of 26 witnesses who will be interrogated
within the next months. Former Chancellor Helmut Kohl is amongst them. CDU
is confident that Kohl, as announced before Christmas, will justify himself
to the committee. However, nothing is sure.

<H1>
CDU/CSU financial expert commits suicide
</H1>
Wolfgang Huellen, employee of the CDU/CSU fraction committed suicide. He
was leader of the "Budget and Finances" Department for several years.
CDU/CSU fraction rejects initial assumptions that the suicide is connected
to the donations scandal. Huellen left a suicide note, the content of
which, however, is still kept secret by police and CDU/CSU fraction.

<P>
This evening it became known that Wolfgang Huellen was under investigations
because of alleged disloyalty. This was confirmed by the Berlin prosecuting
attorney.

<H1>
Preliminary proceedings against Schleusser discontinued
</H1>
Duesseldort prosecuting attorneys discontinued preliminary proceedings
against North-Rhine Westphalian Finance Minister Schleusser. Schleusser was
accussed of offending official secrets; allegedly he had given away an
upcoming investigation of suspected tax evasion of the Westdeutsche
Landesbank to bank chairman Neuber. Yesterday, Schleuser was incriminated
by Sabine Wichmann, the widow of the headpilot of a charter airline.

<H1>
Pension negotiations: secure pension for 30 yrs
</H1>
Coalition and opposition want to work on a pension scheme for the next 30
years. After the first round of negotiations in Berlin, experts from SPD,
The Greens, CDU/CSU, and FDP agreed to the timeframe of 2030 which was
demanded mainly by the opposition. Disputed questions of basic security and
pension adjustements beginning in 2002 will be discussed later.

</H1>

<H1>
Helmut Hofer permitted to leave Iran
</H1>
German businessman Helmut Hofer, who has been captured in Iran, can finally
leave the country. A court in Teheran fined Hofer today for alleged assault
of a policeman to around DM 13.000. Hofer has spent two years in Iranian
jails. In 1997, he was initially sentenced to death for having had an
illegal sexual relationship to a muslim woman.

<H1>
ICE-disaster due to overused steel tire
</H1>
The Juni 1998 ICE-disaster in Eschede with a 101 death-toll was due to
over-exertion of a steel tire. This became evident from a report released
today by the Lueneburg prosecutors attorney.

<H1>
Film of oil on river Rhine dissolved
</H1>
The 13 km long and 100 m wide film of oil on the river Rhine which had
formed yesterday between  Karlsruhe and Leimersheim, dissolved on its own.
According to the waterprotection police, no environmental damage was done.

<H1>
Days for qualifications for soccer world championship
</H1>
German National Soccer league will start its qualification for the world
championship 2002 in Japan and South Corea with a home game against Greece
on September, 2, and an away game against England on Oktober, 7. This was
agreed today by the German Soccer Association who met with the opponents
Greece, England, Albania, and Finland.

<H1>
Basler injured
</H1>
Shock for 1.FC Kaiserslautern: midfield player Mario Basler injured himself
this morning in Kaiserslautern. He has a teared ligament, and will likely
not be available to the national league soccer team for several weeks.
Basler has done only 5 games for the "red devils" since his move to the
Palatinate.
