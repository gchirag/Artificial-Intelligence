<H1>
Rescued miner's physical condition satisfactory
</H1>
The medical staff of the Graz hospital said the miner rescued
10 days after a mine shaft collapsed near Lassing was physically
well but suffering severe psychological consequences. The 24 year
old man was now undergoing psychological treatment. He is not yet
aware of the fate of the remaining 10 miners buried by the
collapse. The doctors said the young man was suffering from slight
dehydration. He would therefore remain under intensive care until
Wednesday. His general state was considered satisfactory by the
doctors. The miner had been rescued last night from a level
60 m below the surface. The search for 10 rescuers who had
themselves been buried by a collapsing shaft is still going
on. After the rescue of the miner, the Austrian authorities are being
charged
with bad coordination. The rescue team from Kassel
said that otherwise survivors might have been rescued within
a week after the disaster.

<H1>
Case against Lopez and his staff dismissed
</H1>
The Darmstadt regional court confirmed the dismissal of the case
against former automobile manager Lopez and 3 of his immediate
staff. The 4 accused will have to shell out considerable sums
to social institutions. Lopez, for instance, will have to
pay DM 400,000. The executives were accused of having purloined
secret business files on their changeover from Opel to VW.
The court stressed that both the prosecution and the accused
agreed to the dismissal of the case.

<H1>
Daimler and Nissan plan joint venture in light truck production
</H1>
Daimler Benz and Nissan, Japans second largest auto maker, plan
a joint venture with the aim to produce light utilitarian
vehicles. Daimler declared that together with Nissan and its
truck building affiliate Nissan Diesel they would develop a
light truck for Asia, South America and borderline developing
countries. Production is planned to start by 2002 in existing
Daimler and Nissan Diesel plants.

<H1>
Father of missing Manuel Schadwald arrested
</H1>
Reiner Wolf, father of Manuel Schadwald, who has been missing for five
years, was arrested in Berlin today. According to the police,
2 warrants for his arrest have been issued. It is not clear as yet
whether these warrants are connected to the recent child porno
scandal in the Netherlands. Wolf had been arrested last night
following a TV program in which he had defended himself against
the accusation of having handed his son over to a Dutch brothel.
He also denied speculations about his alleged connections to the
international child porno scene.

<H1>
CSU sees itself largely in accord with CDU on alien policies
</H1>
According to the spokesman for internal policies of the CSU
Bundestag fraction, Zeitelmann, his party has not given in to CDU on
alien policies. The important point, he said in an interview to radio
"Deutschlandfunk", is that the Union parties' platform
speaks out against increased immigration and in favor of reducing
the present level. The details, he said. were not important. In all cases,
refugees would have to return to their countries of
origin on cessation of the conflicts that had caused their flight.

Krefeld pensioner confesses to bomb threats against railway
</H1>
Police elucidated a series of bomb threats against the German
railway company. A 61 year old Krefeld pensioner confessed to
having phoned in bomb threats all over the country. His action
was motivated by hatred against the state, said the severly
handicapped man. He has up to now been linked to threats received
by Stuttgart, Ulm, Augsburg and Regensburg railway stations.

Churches call for conservationist behavior
</H1>
The 2 major Christian churches put out a call in favor of
ecologically conservationist behavior. The demand was voiced
by the Evangelical Church in Germany and the Roman Catholic
Conference of German Bishops. The statements were issued in
preparation for a "Week for Life", jointly planned by both
churches. The action means to help developing a way of life
less consumption-oriented, the churches say.

<H1>
Gerz withdraws his draft for Holocaust memorial
</H1>
Plastic artist Jochen Gerz withdrew his draft proposal for
a Holocaust memorial in Berlin. According to Gerz, as a result
of the discussions over the last few months official support
for the project has practically disappeared. Intellectuals
and politicians seem to have reached a consensus against
building a memorial for the murdered Jews, he says. This is
due, in the artist's opinion, to a majority trend in public
opinion.

Summer's end promotions start with sales upturn
</H1>
According to an inquiry carried out by the German Retailers' Association,
price cuts of up to 70% brought a steep upturn

with the initial results of the sales promotions.
