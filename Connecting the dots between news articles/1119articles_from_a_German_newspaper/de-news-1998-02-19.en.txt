<H1>
BND: details about Iraq's armoury
</H1>
According to German newspapers the German Foreign Intelligence Agency
(BND) has solid evidence that Iraq has several tonnes of biological and
chemical weapons, most of which are stored on the Tigris-peninsula
Salman Pak (sp?). The coordinator of the secret services, the Minister
of State Bernd Schmidbauer, stated that there were also clear
indications that Iraq had laboratories in which more of these agents
could be manufactured.

<H1>
Caesar: no to Schaeuble's ideas about the "grand bugging attack"
</H1>
The Minister of Justice in Rhineland-Palatinate, Caesar, rejected ideas
of the parliamentary leader of the Conservatives, Wolfgang Schäuble, 
not
to exempt certain professions from the "grand bugging attack" in the
future. Caesar told the ZDF [national TV channel] that this would be a
drawback the Liberals could never agree to  after Wolfgang Schaeuble 
had
argued in favour of the original ideas where there were no exemptions.
The mediation Committee of the two houses of parliament will meet on 2
March to discuss legislation necessary for bugging private homes for
prosecution purposes.

<H1>
Certificates for incomplete apprenticeships
</H1>
The Minister for education, Juergen Ruettgers, and craft unions have
decided that those who give up an apprenticeship or fail the final 
exams
will from now on receive a certificate which will list the relevant
qualifications an apprentice has gained during his apprenticeship.
The latest report on education states that the crisis in vocational
training is far from over. At the end of 1997 more than 23,000
applicants were still looking for an apprenticeship.

<H1>
Aldi-blackmailer: 3 years in prison
</H1>
A 26-year old radio- and TV engineer who had threatened to poison
products unless the food-chain Aldi handed over DM 1 Mio has been
sentenced to 3 years in prison. He was arrested on the third payment
after the police located his radio equipment. The sentence was kept on
the lower end of possible punishments, because the confession of the
accused had given an impression of being still quite immature and his
confession had shown signs of remorse, one judge explained. On the 
other
hand the accused had displayed a high degree of criminal energy.

<H1>
PKK membership in Germany on the increase
</H1>
A report of the Turkish army claims that more and more German nationals 
join the banned Kurdish Worker's Party, PKK. According to general 
Doban,
commander of the province Diabakir, one female PKK fighter from Germany
had to stand trial in a state security court. The General stated that
Turkey has obviously failed to portrait the situation realistically in
Germany.

<H1>
Warrant of arrest against Ex-Minister of Transport
</H1>
A spokesman of the CDU [conservative party] district Rostock confirmed
that a warrant of arrest has been issued against the former Minister of
Transport, Mr. Krause. However, there was no criminal background, and
the warrant should simply force Mr. Krause to sign a sworn statement
regarding his DM 70,000 in rent arrears. The CDU wants to discuss the
situation with Mr. Krause, who is the CDU candidate for the Rostock
constituency in the next federal elections.

Nagano: Two further gold medals for German athletes
</H1>
The German womens' team has won two further gold medals at the Olympics
in Nagano. Uschi Disl, Martina Zellner, Katrin Apel, and Martina Behle

Hilde Gerg from Lengries won the gold medal in the slalom competition.
