<H1>
Kvaerner group will sell shipbuilding companies
</H1>
The British-Norwegian Kvaerner group is going to withdraw world-wide from
the shipbuilding industry and cut approximately 25,000 jobs. A statement to
that effect from the company's board of directors in Oslo was confirmed by
the Kvaerner shipyard in Warmemuende. Also, terms of sale negotiations for
the shipbuilding company in Warmemuende are currently underway. By May 6th,
decisions on this matter are expected to have been reached. In any case the
shipyard will have a new owner. The approximately 1500 jobs at the shipyard
are not in any danger, however.

<H1>
Eichel dismisses Lafontaine confidants
</H1>
The new Minister of Finance Hans Eichel, intends to appoint World Bank
manager Koch-Weser and tax lawyer Zizelsberger as tenured Secretaries of
State. This was confirmed today by the ministry in Bonn. They will replace
the present Secretaries of State Flassbeck and Noe, who were known to be in
close confidence with Mr. Eichel's predecessor Oskar Lafontaine. Both
Parliamentary State Secretaries, Hendriks and Diller, will retain their
positions.

<H1>
Viking-Youth remain banned
</H1>
The right-extremist Viking Youth remain banned. The Federal Administrative
Court in Berlin ruled that the organisation shows close similarities to the
Hitler Youth, and members of the organisation have both acted and expressed
themselves in an anti-constitutional, racist and anti-semitic manner. With
this decision the judges deny the Viking Youth's (founded in 1952) appeal
against a banning order imposed by the Ministry of Internal Affairs five
years ago.

<H1>
North Rhine-Westphalia lays complaint against caging of hens
</H1>
The Federal Administrative Court in Karlsruhe has heard today arguments in
proceedings against the caging of hens. The plaintive, the state of North
Rhine-Westphalia, is going after more natural conditions that are
appropriate for keeping hens. However, at the hearing a federal government
representative opposed an isolated German approach. Bonn wants to try and
reach a EU-wide regulation. The Federal Administrative Court is expected to
reach a decision in a month.

<H1>
Popular initiative in Berlin against spelling reform
</H1>
The Berlin State government has allowed that a popular initiative against
the spelling reform will take place, as demanded by a Berlin based
association. According to the State government spokesman, Mr Butz, the
popular initiative will be held between May 10 and July 9. In this period
of time, Berlin citizens will be able to sign into certain lists to second
the initiative. If at least 243,000 sign such an initiative, a referendum
on the spelling reform will be held.

<H1>
4 deaths in crash of military helicopter
</H1>
A crash of a Bundeswehr (German Armed Forces) helicopter at the Berlin
Airport Tegel has caused the death of four soldiers. The Defense ministry
in Bonn confirmed the accident. According to a spokesperson, the helicopter
crashed while approaching for landing at the military part of the airport,
and then caught fire. The reasons for the accident were still unknown. According
to the spokesperson, the helicopter was on a training flight.

<H1>
Ruling against Konstantin Wecker quashed
</H1>
The case against singer-songwriter Konstantin Wecker for possessing illegal
drugs will have to be tried again. The Munich Prosecutor's Office confirmed
that the Bavarian Regional High Court set aside the sentence of the
Munich Regional Court that had convicted the 51-year old Mr Wecker to 2 1/2
years in prison. According to the Regional High Court, the Munich Regional
Court did not consider appropriately whether the artist was at least
partially unresponsible.
