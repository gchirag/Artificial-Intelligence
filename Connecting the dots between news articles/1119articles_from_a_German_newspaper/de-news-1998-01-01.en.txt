<H1>
Employers demand heavier cuts in social security
</H1>
Employers demand heavier cuts in the social security system
than are to date planned by the government. Chairman of
Employers' Association Hundt said today that public social security
organizations in Germany have grown into a paralyzing
catchall welfare system that is no longer financially bearable.
Hundt demanded a rapid reduction in pension levels. Public
health benefits should be limited to the medical essentials.

<H1>
Bluem plans on government subsidy for low-income pensioners
</H1>
=46ederal minister for labor Bluem pretends to subsidize private
 pension plans for low income population with government funds.
According to the daily "Handelsblatt", Bluem is working out
an amendment to the "Vermoegensbildungsfoerderung" (a program
to help and encourage people to build nest-eggs) that would
help low-income persons to offset the reduction of social security
pensions with private pension plans. The asset building program
is to be increased and to cover all citizens above 16 years of age.
Among other provisions, the limit of subsidized amounts
is to be increased from the present DM 936 to DM 1,200.

<H1>
Skinhead attack on home for disabled injures several persons
</H1>
=46our skinheads injured several inmates of a home for the physically
disabled in Saxony-Anhalt. According to police,
one of the orderlies of the home caused the melee. He allegedly
hit one of the female companions of the skinheads in the face,
from which a bad free-for-all resulted. The 15 to 24 year olds
started beating up the inmates and broke up the furniture.
A deaf-mute suffered a brain concussion from a bottle on his head.
Another disabled man was kicked in the scrotal parts and had to be
hospitalized.

<H1>
Food poisoning among inmates of home for aged
</H1>
26 inmates of a senior citizens' home in Munich suffered food
poisoning. 6 of them had to be hospitalized. The others received on-site
medical treatment. Immediately after lunch, they suffered attacks of
diarrhea and vomiting. The exact origin of the poisoning has not yet
been established. There seems to be no peril of life.

<H1>
New Year ski jumping contest in Garmisch-Partenkirchen
</H1>
Japan's Kasoyoshi Funati repeated his success in Obersdorf and won
the New Year jumping contest in Garmisch-Partenkirchen in his second
victory in the 4 towers' tournament. Michael Wagner on
place 7 was the highest-ranking German. Dieter Thoma came in 8th.
