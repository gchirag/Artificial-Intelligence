<H1>
Schroeder intends to push ahead with austerity measures
</H1>
Chancellor Gerhard Schroeder intends to push ahead with the government's
planned austerity measures. "The program may not be toppled" said Mr.
Schroeder in a television interview. He continued that the program had to
be presented to both the lower and upper houses of parliament and that
obstacles would become insurmountable if there was any doubt in the
proposed measures. Mr. Schroeder was convinced that the economy would
improve in the second half of the year and that unemployment figures
would decline.

<H1>
FDP: nearing the SPD?
</H1>
The FDP could become more interested in the SPD as a possible coalition
partner if Chancellor Schroeder managed to reform the party as planned,
said Guido Westerwelle, Secretary-General of the FDP said yesterday to
the "Frankfurter Rundschau" newspaper. He continued that it would be
exciting if the SPD were to follow a "Blair" type of course. Mr.
Westerwelle said that he was watching developments with interest, in
particular what direction the German Social Democrats would now take.

<H1>
Klimmt plans to re-introduce tax on capital
</H1>
State Premier of the Saarland Klimmt plans to reintroduce tax on capital.
The SPD politician said to the newspaper "Bild am Sonntag" that private
capital in Germany had increased by 56 billion marks between 1991 and
1997. Capital taxes had only been one percent. He said this would be an
indication of his plans but that he was open to suggestion.

<H1>
Tougher speed limits due to summer smog
</H1>
German motorists will have to become accustomed to stricter speed limits
in summer smog in the future. This was announced by Juergen Trittin,
Minister of the Environment to the "Sueddeutschen Zeitung" newspaper. He
said that an EU regulation would soon be passed that would rule that
ozone levels may not rise above 24 mg per cubic meter of air and that
this could only be achieved by stricter speed limits.

<H1>
Chamber of Doctors: patient care guaranteed
</H1>
Joerg-Dietrich Hoppe, president of the Federal Chamber of Doctors, said
that so-called "Hardship Program" for medical aid doctors would not
endanger patient care. Patients would continue to be provided with
medically necessary treatment, he said to "Die Welt" daily newspaper and
that there was absolutely no "rationing" involved in the program. He
continued that the program aimed at avoiding doctors reacting in "panic"
when their medical-aid budgets had been exceeded.

<H1>
Unsuccessful police hunt for murderer
</H1>
The alleged murderer of Remagen, Dieter Zurwehme, has managed to avoid
capture once again. Police were not able to find the suspect after
searching a 20 by 250 metre bushy compound in Bueckeburg in Lower Saxony
on Sunday afternoon. Investigators had seen a man who fitted the police
description on the grounds. The fifty-seven-year old had also just
managed to avoid capture the afternoon before in Maisfeld.

<H1>
Cooperation between Telekom and Telefonica
</H1>
The German telephone corporation Telekom and their Spanish counterpart
Telefonica are apparently planning a cooperation agreement. According to
the "Frankfurter Zeitung" newspaper, the two corporations plan to merge.
Telefonica had, however, denied a planned merger only a week ago.

<H1>
Annual Wagner festival in Bayreuth
</H1>
Spectators applauded wildly after the first performance of Lohengrin at
the annual Wagner Festival at Bayreuth. This is "high season" for Wagner
fans from all over the world. The festival will run until August 28
comprising 30 performances in all. Tickets have long been sold out.

<H1>
Erik Zabel wins Green Jersey in Tour de France
</H1>
Erik Zabel has won the Green Jersey for best sprinter in the Tour de
France for the third time in a row. However, he did not manage to win the
last stage and came second after Australian Robby Mc Ewen. The American
Lance Armstrong was the overall winner.
