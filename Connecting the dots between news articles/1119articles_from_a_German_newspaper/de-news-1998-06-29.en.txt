<H1>
Earthquake aid for Turkey
</H1>
The German government is extending DM 2 million in aid to
Turkey to be used in recent earthquake areas. A team of the
ministry for development aid will coordinate the application
in the affected region.

<H1>
Wage scale agreement for North Rhine-Westphalia's retail trade
</H1>
The wage quarrel in North Rhine Westphalia's retail trade was
ended by a new agreement between unions and employers. The
agreement provides for a 2.1% raise in wages effective as of
June 1st, 1998. In addition, low income employees will get
raises above the average. Apprentices will receive an additional
DM 12 per month during the first 2 years of apprenticeship and
DM 15 during the third year. It was agreed that no increases
will be paid for April and May. The agreement in Germany's
largest tariff region has a validity of 12 months. Leaderships
of unions and employers have a July 13th deadline to decide on
sanctioning this compromise solution. The outcome of the wage
talks is on a level with agreements in other regions.

<H1>
Telekom launches local tariff calls for D1-Net
</H1>
The Deutsche Telekom is offering local tariffs in its D1-Net
as of late August. Customers can make a choice of an area
where they will pay a daytime rate of DM .68 instead of DM 1.89
a minute. In densely populated regions this will be the area
covered by a 4-digit area code; in rural areas the first 4 digits
will identify a local tariff region. The new tariffs are valid
for interconnection with the fixed net.

<H1>
German soccer hooligan arrested
</H1>
A German hooligan who had attacked and injured a Brazilian
TV-cameraman during the Lens world championship match on June 21st
was arrested by Kaiserslautern police today. During the
interrogation he confessed. He had been recognized by policemen
from a TV newscast.

<H1>
Tennis: Kiefer drops out in Wimbledon
</H1>
For the first time in 17 years there will be no German players
in Wimbledon's eighth finals. The last of an original number of
17 German tennis pros, Nikolas Kiefer, was beaten by Richard
Krajizek 4:6, 6:7 and 6:7.
