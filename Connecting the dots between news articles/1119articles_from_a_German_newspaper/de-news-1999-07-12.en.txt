<H1>
Differing reactions to Beck's proposal
</H1>
Chancellor Schroeder doesn't want to get involved in the debate over
wage and salary increases.  Employers welcomed Mainz government head
Beck's proposal to only allow wage and salary increases to rise over
the next two years along with the rate of inflation.  Heavy criticism
came from the labor unions.  The past has shown that moderate wage
agreements have been no guarantee of jobs.  The Ministry of Labor has
asserted that the proposals are reasonable.

<H1>
Kosovo refugees to be flown home starting Thursday
</H1>
The UN Refugee Aid Organization has announced a sweift return of
Kosovo refugees sent to Western Europe, the US and Australia,
following a conference in Geneva.  Up to 1000 refugees per day are to
be flown home beginning Thursday; higher numbers were deemed
impossible due to the overburdened airport in Skopie, Macedonia.
Germany, Belgium, the Netherlands, and Switzerland have demanded a
higher capacity as soon as the airport in Pristina is again in
operation.  According to UN reports, about 136,000 people, mostly
Serbs, have fled since the entry of the KFOR units four weeks ago.

State social security institutions to merge
</H1>

protests from both of them. According to dpa information, the CDU/FDP

<H1>
Teacher moves to Berlin over scarf dispute
</H1>
The muslim teacher no longer allowed to teach in Stuttgart because she
wears a head scarf, is evidently moving to Berlin.  SWF has
information that she will move to a private Islamic school.  The
superior school board refused to admit the teacher because of her
scarf, which symbolizes cultural separation.  The woman is sueing the
state; a verdict is expected in fall.
