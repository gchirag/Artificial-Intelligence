<H1>
Germany has a new head of state
</H1>
Johannes Rau was sworn in today as Germany's eighth president. In his
inaugaral speech, the former prime minister of North Rhine Westphalia
described his goals of his period of office.  Rau wants to support
solidarity and tolerance in the society. He wants to play a double role: he
will speak for all Germans on one hand, and wants to help minorities to
gain attention on the other hand.

<H1>
Bundestag brings its residence in Bonn to an end
</H1>
Today the German Bundestag had its last plenary meeting in Bonn. On this
occassion, former Chancellor Helmut Kohl gave his first speech since his
voting out last September. Kohl thanked the city of Bonn for its "servive
to the nation". He said that the freest, most humane, and most social state
which German soil has ever seen was created in Bonn. At noon, Bundestag and
Bundesrat members assembled for a joint meeting during which Johannes Rau
was sworn in.

<H1>
Catholic consultation of pregnant women in conflict under discussion
</H1>
The Federal Government and its Laender talked today about the role of
the catholic consultation of pregnant women in conflict. The core question
was whether the consultation certificates with the addition of it being
prohibited would allow a legal abortion. The Federal Government and its
Laender do not see any illegalities. Bavaria, Baden-Wuerttemberg, and
Saxony had no problems with the new consultation certificates. Other
Laenders, however, doubt unbiased consultations by the catholic church.
This will be now clarified with the bishops.

<H1>
Martin Bangemann takes leave of absence as EU commissioner
</H1>
Martin Bangemann was given leave of absence as EU-commissioner. The
FDP-EU-politician was in charge of industry politics and telecommunication.
Yesterday he asked to be released from his duties as soon as possible
because he wants to work for the Spanish company Telefonica. This caused
annoyance in the EU-commision as Bangemann changes into excatly that area
for which he had been responsible for for many years.

<H1>
Further attacks on Turkish facilities
</H1>
Further attacks on Turkish facilities were registered last night also in
cities in North Rhine Westphalia and Baden-Wuerttemberg. Damage was caused
but no people were harmed. The attackers0 are most likely followers of the
illegal Turkish workers' party PKK. Two days ago, Turkey sentenced their
leader Oecalan to death. The sentence is as yet not final.

<H1>
Dismissal without notice due to rassism authorised
</H1>
The Federal Court for Work has decided that a dismissal without notice due
to rassism is permissible. The case is about an apprentice of the Berlin
municipal cleaning department who put a provoking sign on the desk of a
Turkish collegue; together with others he sang neonazi songs. The response
of the Berlin municipal cleaning department was his dismissal without any
advance notice - rightly, as the Federal Court decided today.

<H1>
Arrest in connection with Merseburg bombing
</H1>
Three days after the bombing in Merseburg, police arrested a main suspect
in Poland. The 31-year-old German is now in jail in Breslau. A request for
extradiction has come in. According to the police, the man is a nightclub
owner and head of a motorcycle gang. The bombing of the Merseburg
restaurant injured 20 people, partly severly. Investigations focus on
circles of organised crime, red-light milieu, and bikers.

<H1>
Joyful swimming in Bavaria
</H1>
The swimming bans implemented after the Whitsunday floods have mostly been
removed. According to Health Minister Stamm, the situation has much
improved. She said, test would give good results of the water quality. A
swimming ban exists only for a few more lakes.

<H1>
Steffi Graf in semi-final in Wimbledon
</H1>
Steffi Graf reached the semi-final in the tennis tournaments of Wimbledon
for the tenth time. Graf beat the American Venus Williams 6:3, 2:6 und 6:4.
Graf will play the quarter-final against the winner of the match between
the French Natalie Tauziat and the Kroat Lucic. With a win against the
title holder Jana Novotna,  the American Lindsay Davenport also qualified
for the semi-finals.

<H1>
Gold for Germany
</H1>
The German equipe celebrates the 18th win in a sequence of the dressage
world championships in Arnheim/The Netherlands. Gold medals went to Nadine
Kapellmann, Alexandra Simonsderidder, Ulla Salzgeber and Isabel Wert. The
Dutch team had, again, to be happy with the second place.
