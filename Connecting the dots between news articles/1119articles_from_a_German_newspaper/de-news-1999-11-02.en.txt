<H1>
Another victim of Bad Reichenhall shooting
</H1>
After a 16-year-old ran amok in Bad Reichenhall yesterday, a fifth
victim, a 59-year-old man, died of his serious shot wounds in a
Salzburg hospital today. Yesterday, the youth shot dead two
neighbours and injured several passers-by. After running amok, the
young man killed his sister, 18, with five shots and then himself. The
police are still unclear about the motive.

<H1>
Schroeder arrived in Shanghai
</H1>
Starting his three-day official visit of China, German Chancellor
Gerhard Schroeder has arrived in the harbour city Shanghai. There, he
is going to visit the sites of German companies. Thursday, Schroeder
will continue to Beijing where he will meet members of the Chinese
government. The Chancellor arrived from Japan where he had suggested
including China in the medium-term in the group of leading industrial
countries. The group, also known as the G8 countries, comprises
currently eight countries.

<H1>
Eid acquitted again
</H1>
Almost four years after the fire attack on an asylum seekers' home in
Luebeck, Schleswig-Holstein, the Lebanese Safwan Eid has been acquitted
again in after an appeal. Even after an evaluation of phone tap records
that had not been used so far, the Kiel Regional Court did not find any
evidence that the 23-year-old was guilty.
In January 1996 ten people died and 38 were injured in the fire.

<H1>
USA demand higher compensation for NS forced labourers
</H1>
In the negotiations for the compensation of former Nazi forced
labourers, the USA have called on both sides to make concessions.
Commissioner Bindenagel said in Washington that he expected Germany to
raise its offer of 6 billion DM. At the same time, the representatives
of the Nazi victims should show some flexibility.

<H1>
North German states want closer collaboration
</H1>
The five North German states want to collaborate more closely and so
form a counterweight to the Southern states. This was announced by the
five social democratic heads of state of Hamburg, Lower Saxony, Bremen,
Schleswig-Holstein and Mecklenburg-Vorpommern. During their meeting in
Luebeck, they decided that in future the North German states should form
a united front in parliament. Apart from that, traffic projects will be
organised jointly. The Northern states plan to appear together at
international trade fairs as well.

<H1>
Arrest warrant against 19-year-old suspect for triple murder
</H1>
The Koblenz state court has issued an arrest warrant for a 19-year-old
for a triple murder. The young man is said to have murdered his parents
and sister. He has already confessed to killing his parents with a knife
and an axe in Urbar, near Koblenz, last week. He also confessed to
disposing of his sister's body, but blamed her death on his father.
The Koblenz police has not, as yet, been able to establish a motive.
According to their investigations so far, the family concerned was a
completely inconspicuous one.

<H1>
More drug related deaths in Baden-Wuerttemberg
</H1>
In Baden-Wuerttemberg the number of drug related deaths is increasing.
Information gathered by the Schwarzwalder newspaper states that in the
first ten months of this year, 228 people died of as a result of their
addiction. According to the newspaper that is 57 more drug deaths, than
in the same time-frame last year in Baden-Wuerttemberg. The state
capital, Stuttgart, has contributed considerably to the negative trend.
