<H1>
Schroeder comments on crisis in Iraq
</H1>
German Chancellor Gerhard Schroeder has said that Saddam Hussein
should be made aware of the consequences of renewed provocation. Mr.
Schroeder said that Mr. Hussein should immediately cooperate fully with
the United Stations,without any preconditions. Joschka Fischer, Minister
of Foreign Affairs, plans to working towards persuading the international
community to lift sanctions against Iraq.

<H1>
"Alliance for Work" talks resume
</H1>
The latest round "Alliance for Work" talks were resumed again
today. This round of meetings together with representatives from the
government and employer and employee associations was chaired by Mr.
Hombach, Minister for the Chancellor's Office. A so-called steering
committee whose task it will be to coordinate top-level talks, is to
resume its function. The next round of top-level talks will take place at
the end of February. Bernhard Jagoda, president for the Federal Labour
Office, predicts that unemployment numbers can be reduced by one million
unemployed over the next four years.

Struck admits that government has made mistakes
</H1>
Mr. Struck, parliamentary leader of the SPD, has admitted that the
current coalition govermernt have made mistakes. He said to the daily
newspaper "Die Welt" that the artificial time pressure they had set
themselves was responsible for this. According to Mr. Struck, "the motor
is just beginning to run smoothly". The Green and SPD parliamentary
factions have to learn that the government should no longer be attacked,
but rather supported, continued Mr. Struck.

<H1>
Ruhrgas AG purchases shares in Russian gas monopoly
</H1>
The German gas company, Ruhrgas AG, has purchased shares in the
Russian gas monopoly, Gasprom. Representatives of both concerns will sign
the contract in Moscow on Monday. Ruhrgas will pay over a billion  marks
for 2,5 percent of Gasprom shares. The company, based in Essen, is
Gasprom's largest client. The two concerns had signed an agreement only
in May that Gasprom will continue to supply Ruhrgas with 25 billion marks
worth of gas until the year 2020.

<H1>
Germany for communal EU strategy in dealing with Russia
</H1>
The German government plans to implement a communal EU strategy in
dealing with Russia in their period of EU presidency. The new program
will be organised according to a program of points and a list on concrete
expectations as far as Russian foreign politics is concerned. Other
points are food aid, advice in the banking sector and closer cooperation
in specific regions.

<H1>
Poppe: no more refugees to be deported to Algeria
</H1>
Mr. Poppe, the German government's special representative for human
rights, has demanded that no more refugees be deported to Algeria. The
North-African state is no longer able to protect those threatened by
attacks, said Mr. Poppe to the "Frankfurter Rundschau" newspaper and that
a big problem is that Germany does not recognise persecution by non-state
agencies as valid grounds for granting asylum.

<H1>
Still no clues regarding gravestone desecration
</H1>
The police still have no leads on the culprits, twenty four hours
after the desecration of the gravestone Mr. Galinksky, late chairman of
the Central Jewish Committee in Germany. Unknown perpetrators had
exploded a bomb on the memorial slab covering Mr. Galinksky's grave in
the Berlin suburb of Charlottenburg yesterday. It is the second attack on
the grave in the last few months. German president Roman Herzog condemned
the attack. Mr. Bubis, president of the Central Jewish Committee, said
that this attack was an attack on the Jewish community in Germany.

<H1>
German Rail plans to cut InterRegio links
</H1>
According to a report in the newspaper "Die Welt", German Rail
have plans to either cut or severely limit InterRegio links from May
30th. This will affect train connections between Karlsruhe and Salzburg
as well as between Saarbruecken and Lindau. German Rail tracks will also
be more closely guarded  using additional officers from the German
Federal Guard after a series of attacks on railway lines.
