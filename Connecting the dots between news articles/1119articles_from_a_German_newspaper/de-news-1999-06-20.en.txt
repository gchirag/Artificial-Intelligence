<H1>
Serb military pullout complete
</H1>
The Serb military pullout from Kosovo is now complete. This was confirmed
by a spokesman for the French KFOR units who said that the Serbs have
left Kosovo. The French troops control the Northern sector which was the
last to be cleared. NATO had given the Serbian troops an ultimatum that
expired at midnight tonight (Sunday). At the same time as the Serbs are
retreating, more and more Kosovo Albanians are returning home. According
to the UNHCR refugee agency, numbers have reached one hundred thousand.
KFOR soldiers have discovered more prison camps in the North of Kosovo.
According to British media reports, Serbian soldiers had imprisoned
60,000 Kosovars for weeks near Podijevo. The people were emaciated and
sick.

<H1>
Germany and Russia to cooperate in the rebuilding of Kosovo
</H1>
Germany and Russia plan to cooperate in the rebuilding of
Kosovo, said German Chancellor Gerhard Schroeder after a short working
breakfast with Russian President Boris Yeltsin . Concrete projects have
been discussed, Mr. Schroeder said, although he did not give details. Mr.
Yeltsin said in a short declaration that he is happy with the latest
results of the Kosovo negotiations and that reconciliation is the number
one priority at this time.  Mr. Yeltsin participated in the final day of
the world economic summit. In the afternoon, he held talks about the
situation in Kosovo with US President Clinton.

<H1>
Dispute over pension reform to be settled next week
</H1>
German Chancellor Gerhard Schroeder intends to settle the dispute over
the planned pension reform this coming week. In a TV interview, the
Chancellor seemed to generally approve of a plan to introduce additional
private retirement investment. However, it is not clear whether this
should be mandatory or voluntary. The Green Party, the Christian
Democratic Union (CDU), labor unions and insurance providers reject Mr.
Riester's, Minister of Labor, plan.

<H1>
Bremen: Grand coalition signed and sealed
</H1>
Two weeks after the elections in Bremen, the continuation of the grand
coalition has been signed and sealed. For the most difficult question of
how Bremen will vote in the German Bundesrat (Upper House of Parliament),
the compromise was agreed upon that in partisan questions Bremen will
continue to abstain from the vote. The local Senate as well as the state
parliament will be reduced in size in the coming legislative period. Four
of the seven senators will come from the Social Democratic Party (SPD).

<H1>
Lutheran Church Congress comes to an end
</H1>
The Lutheran Church Congress had ended with an appeal for
lasting peace in Europe. "We ask all people to work towards this end",
Congress President Ms. Rinke told the 60,000 participants at the final
service in the Gottlieb-Daimler stadium. The President of the Lutheran
Church Mr. Kock said that the Congress as a success and that many people
had regained the taste for religious belief in Stuttgart. There were more
than 100,000 participants at the Congress every day.

<H1>
Tax breaks for single parents to be revised
</H1>
CDU Chairman Mr. Schaeuble has insisted that tax breaks for single
parents be revised after the judgment of the Federal Constitutional
Court. Given tight public budgets, the requirements of the ruling will
not be met by extending tax breaks for single parents to married couples.
The court, in Mr. Schaeuble's opinion, only demands equal rights for
married and unmarried parents.
