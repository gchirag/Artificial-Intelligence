<H1>
Berlin foreign ministers' meeting stands by human rights
</H1>
Participants of the European-Asiatic foreign ministers'
conference in Berlin agreed to strengthen political
dialogue. A joint declaration names monetary stability,
environmental protection and curbing the spread of weapons
of mass destruction as primary aims. EU participants
gave the 10 Asian states represented good notes in their
effort to surmount the financial crisis in their area.
In a final document, all participants reaffirmed their
support of the UN and the declarations of human right,
although China's foreign minister reaffirmed his country's
stand refusing intervention in its internal affairs.

<H1>
VIAG - algroup merger fell through
</H1>
The pretended merger between Munich's VIAG corporation
and the Swiss algroup seems to have fallen through.
Reportedly, a mutual compromise on the respective market
value of both companies could not be reached. The merger
would have resulted in Germany's sixth largest
industrial corporation.

<H1>
Wage agreement in banking sector
</H1>
In the matter of a wage contract for the 470,000 bank
workers a breakthrough seems finally to have occurred.
According to a spokesman of the employers' association
in Frankfurt/Main said negotiators had reached an
agreement. It was said to be mainly based on the
employers proposal and consists of a one time payment
of DM 350.00 for January through March and a straight
3.1% raise from there on.
The workers union's chief negotiator Renner qualified
the announcement, saying that the matter had still to
be discussed by the labor side.

<H1>
North Rhine-Westphalia's retailers start wage negotiations
</H1>
In the first round of wage talks for North Rhine-Westphalia's
450,000 retail store workers, unions are demanding a 6%
wage increase. The Wuppertal talks will serve as a model
for the entire sector.

<H1>
Radioactive water leak from closed down research reactor
</H1>
Radioactive water seeped into the ground and into a river
nearby at a deactivated research reactor in the Rhenania
town of Juelich. North Rhine-Westphalia's ministry for
the economy in Duesseldorf issued a statement to the effect
that according to the measurements made by the supervisory
authority TUeV, emissions stayed within the legal limit.

Mysterious coating on cars turns up
</H1>
A mysterious coating was discovered on about 100 cars in
Wiesbaden's Amoeneburg district. Both its origin and
possible toxicity are as yet unknown.
