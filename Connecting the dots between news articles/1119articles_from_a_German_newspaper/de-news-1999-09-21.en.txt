<H1>
First German helpers on the way to Taiwan
</H1>
After the heavy earthquake in Taiwan the first German helpers are on
their way into the disaster area. Among the helpers are search dog
teams and technical rescuers. Several countries including Germany,
Japan, and the U.S.A. have offered financial aid. The earthquake was
the strongest one in Taiwan in this century. It is assumed that at
least 1700 people were killed and more than 4000 were injured.

<H1>
Federal Labour Minister Riester wants to back private pension plans
</H1>
Federal Labour Minister Walter Riester wants to support private pension
schemes. He has suggested that employees earning up to 60.000 DM
annually receive up to 250 DM per year as a government benefit. In
addition, Riester wants to promote early retirement so that those who
retire at age 60 do not have to give up up to 18 percent of their
pension. For the next rounds of collective bargaining Riester wants
unions and employers to set up funds that would cover the loss of
pension. The whole scheme would receive tax benefits.

</H1>
Letters by a nonexistent EU authority demanding the payment of a fee
have now also surfaced in Baden-Wuerttemberg. Unknown impostors have

Central bank confirms positive economic trend
</H1>
The German economy is growing again. The Bundesbank,
Germany's central bank reports this month that since the beginning of
this summer the economy has grown about five percent. Especially
foreign companies have ordered more from German companies since July.
Domestic demand has also risen.

<H1>
Lehmann's chairmanship German bishop's conference confirmed
</H1>
The archbishop of Main, Lehmann, has been reelected to chair the
German bishop's conference. Over the coming days, the bishops at the
conference must decide whether they will comply with the Pope's demands
and withdraw from the Catholic discussion on the pregnancy conflict.
Lehmann is assuming that the Church will decide on the withdrawal. He
also said that, in the future, the proof of mandatory counseling form
would probably be discontinued. However, he appealed to every bishop to
make a decision regarding his own diocese.

<H1>
Trials against human smugglers begin in Dresden
</H1>
Today, seven men and two women must justify themselves before
the State Court for smuggling people. They stand accused of having
smuggled more than 1000 people over the border between the Czech
Republic and Germany between 1997 and 1998. A number of transports had
to be aborted, since the people in the overloaded lorries nearly
suffocated. They were then simply abandoned on the roadside.
