<H1>
Drop in Cebit Home attendance figures
</H1>
Significantly fewer people have visited the second Cebit Home trade fair
in Hannover. Around 180 000 people attended in the last five days,
whereas 215 000 attended the fair two years ago. However, most of the
exhibitors were still either satisfied or very satisfied with the
numbers. In particular, computer and video game exhibitors had very
positive reactions from the public.

<H1>
Rent increases
</H1>
Many tenants will be faced with rent increases in the next few weeks. The
reason for this is the so-called cut-off rate for dwellings built before
1980 costing over DM 8 per square meter, excluding overheads, no longer
applies as from September 1st.  Rents can now be increased by 30% per
annum instead of only 20% for all buildings concerned.

<H1>
Robberies in Munich
</H1>
Three teenagers robbed two gas stations in Munich on Saturday
night. A masked 16-year-old looted DM 900 at a gas station in the
Bodenseestrasse, although he was caught and arrested at the Pasing S-Bahn
station a while later. Two masked teenage girls also tried to rob a gas
station in the Ingolstadter Strasse at around two in the morning. The garage
attendant did not let himself be intimidated by them and the two girls,
aged thirteen and fifteen, had to flee without loot. They were also
arrested within a short time.

<H1>
Merkel demands guarantees regarding millennium changeover
</H1>
Angela Merkel, Federal Minister of the Environment, has demanded that
nuclear power stations provide proof that their computers are equipped to
deal with the changeover into the year 2000 without problem. A spokesman
for the ministry in Bonn confirmed a newspaper report in this regard. The
international nuclear energy authorities are also investigating the
reliability of computers in nuclear power stations worldwide.

<H1>
Further deportation of Bosnian refugees
</H1>
Berlin's senator for internal affairs, Joerg Schoenbom, has announced
further deportations of Bosnian refugees. The CDU politician said to the
"Berliner Zeitung" newspaper that the time for hospitality has now come
to an end. Mr. Schoenbom assumes that the number of Bosnian refugees in
Berlin will be reduced by around four to five thousand by the end of
December. He predicted that around 15 000 to 16 000 Bosnian refugees will
still be living in Berlin at the end of the year.
