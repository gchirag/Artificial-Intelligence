<H1>
Bundestag approves German participation in Kosovo
</H1>
The parliament in Bonn approved the deployment of Bundeswehr soldiers
in a NATO-led peace force in Kosovo with a large majority. 556 members
voted for it, only 42 against it. There were 10 abstentions. Defense
minister Scharping said the pressure on Serbs and Kosovo-Albanians
must remain to avoid a humanitarian catastrophe which could lead to
more refugees to Western Europe. Foreign Minister Fischer stressed
that peace in Kosovo would be impossible without military guard. He
also said that it is important to have an international force under
NATO command. The former Defense Minister Ruehe mentioned that
military action in Kosovo would take the Bundeswehr to its breaking
point.

<H1>
Budget talks in Bundestag
</H1>
Economy and labor market politics were the focus of budget talks in the
Bundestag. It was stressed that growth factors should be mobilised and mass
unemployment fought. Minister of Economics Mueller justified his budget and
advertised for initiatives of the private industry. Minister of Employment
Riester called the 1999 budget a plan to reduce unemployment. Minister of
Transportation Muentefering stressed that he would support the construction
of the controversial magnetic aerial railway 'Transrapid'.

<H1>
Work team to come up with further steps for tax reform
</H1>
The federal government, unions and employers are planning to come up with a
joint program for further steps of the enterprise and the eco tax reform.
Chancellor Schroeder said the team should come up with a conclusive draft.
The judgement of the Federal Constitutional Court for the betterment of
families would have to be taken into consideration as well. Mr Schroeder
stressed that the labor alliance would be continued, and that the next
meeting is scheduled for mid May.

<H1>
Ministers taking stronger measures against Kurdish rioters
</H1>
The Ministers of the Interior and Justice of the states are planning to take
stronger measures against Kurdish rioters. At a conference in Bonn they
encouraged the Federal Government to have talks with the Turkish Government to
avoid that expelled Kurds are tortured or killed in Turkey. Federal Interior
Minister Otto Schily stressed that an assent in Ankara should be monitored.

<H1>
Massive strikes in public sector
</H1>
Public sector unions expanded their warning strikes one day before the third
round of negotiations in Stuttgart. About 100,000 employees in 150 cities
stopped working for several hours, mainly in North-Rhine Westphalia, Lower
Saxony, Berlin and Brandenburg, above all affecting public local
transportation and garbage collection.

<H1>
Baden-Wuerttemberg metal compromise finds use in NRW
</H1>
The settlement for the metal industry in Baden-Wuerttemberg is supposed to
be adopted by Northrine-Westphalia as well according to an agreement reached
by employers and the metal union in Wuppertal. The Wage Commission of the IG
Metall union and the majority of the regional employer associations have to
agree first.

<H1>
Bishops' views of counseling program undisclosed
</H1>
The views of the Catholic church regarding the counseling program for
pregnant women in conflict remains undisclosed. The result of the
bishops' votes will not be disclosed in the near future, and only after
consultation with Pope John Paul II. Apparently the bishops reached a
compromise in which the consultation certificate will be expanded to
encompass support and legal claims for the pregnant women.

<H1>
France rejects German proposal for EU agricultural reform
</H1>
France rejects the compromise proposal by the German council presidency
for an EU agricultural reform. The French Agriculture Minister Galvany
said in Brussels the plan would burden the European Union with additional
12 billion euro which needed to be charged to the agricultural budget of
the individual states. The EU ministers negotiated today for the fourth
day in a row, expecting to reach an agreement that can be presented
before the EU summit on Friday.

<H1>
32 died in avalanche in Paznaun Valley
</H1>
All persons who wanted to leave the ski resort Galtuer have been evacuated.
Since yesterday, approximately 3,000 persons have been brought out of the
avalanche region. Today alone, about 1,800 holidaymakers and residents were
evacuated by helicopter. More tourists will be evacuated tonight and
tomorrow. 32 people died in the avalanche catastrophe; at least 12 of them
were German. More persons are still missing.
