<H1>
EU defence ministers' conference ends without resolution
</H1>
The defence ministers of the European Union have ended their two-day
conference in Vienna without having come to any resolution. They merely
agreed to work on concepts for a credible military intervention force to
deal with international crises, such as the current one in Kosovo. British
Defence Minister George Robertson criticized what he called the laborious
and slow decision-making in the EU.

<H1>
EU promises membership talks for Latvia
</H1>
The European Commission has given the government of Latvia the prospect of
possibly joining the European Union soon. EU-Commissioner, Hans van den
Broek has told the European Parliament in Brussels that negotiations with
that goal could begin before the end of next year. He said that Latvia had
made great economic and political progress. The same could be said of
Lithuania and Slovakia, he added, but did not specify any date for talks
with those countries. Regarding Turkey, van den Broek said that the
government in Ankara must subordinate its army to democratic controls
before membership in the EU could be considered.

<H1>
New federal government: monetary and economic policy must be linked
</H1>
The SPD-Green Coalition says that monetary policy must also come under
consideration when discussing general economic policies. Government
spokesman, Uwe-Karsten Heye made the statement following a cabinet
meeting, making it clear, however, that no one was questioning the
independence of the German or the European Central Bank. Heye said that,
nevertheless, it must remain possible to discuss monetary policy in
connection with attempts to foster economic growth and effective
employment policies. He also added that the Maastricht Treaty does not
preclude such a tie.

<H1>
Donations for Central American hurricane victims
</H1>
Numerous aid measures have been set up in Germany to help the victims of
hurricane Mitch in Central America. Germans at large have also been called
upon to make their own donations. The federal government has released an
initial sum of 3.7 million DM. The Diakonie, a church-based social welfare
organization, has already promised 250,,000 DM. The UN and the World Bank
have also pledged their support. The United States has sent helicopters
into the disaster area. Cuban and French doctors have arrived in Honduras.
Spain is sending aid materials to Central America by airlift.
Over 10,000 people are still missing, following the storm; the number of
dead has reached 9,000; millions are homeless.

<H1>
Parliamentary Greens want improvements to tax reform
</H1>
Those members of the Green Party in the Bundestag have again called for
improvements to the tax reforms agreed upon with the SPD. Economic
spokesman for the group, Margareta Wolf, said that reducing the rate for
the highest tax bracket from 53 to 48.5 percent was not sufficient. She
also said that more attention had to be paid to making sure that small and
medium businesses were not burdened further.
The deputy-chairman of the SPD's parliamentary group, Ingrid
Matthaeus-Maier, announced that the tax reform bill will be presented next
Tuesday.

<H1>
Steffi Graf reaches quarter-finals in Leipzig tennis tournament
</H1>
Steffi Graf has reached the quarter-finals in the Leipzig tennis
tournament. She beat Japanese player Ai Sugiyama 6:4 and 6:3. Graf's next
opponent will be decided in the match between Arantxa Sanchez-Vicario of
Spain and Anne-Gaelle Sidot of France.
