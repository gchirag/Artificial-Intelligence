<H1>
Kinkel and Ruehe disagree on NATO intervention in Kosovo
</H1>
The federal government continues to be at odds over the possibility
of military intervention by NATO in Kosovo. Foreign Minister Kinkel
stressed that a UN mandate would be necessary for involvement.
According to Defense Minister Ruehe, however, NATO could intervene
without an existing vote by the Security Council. Chancellor Kohl
has not yet commented on the conflict. SPD and Buendnis-Gruene also
consider a UN mandate to be necessary for NATO military action.

<H1>
Russian military representative at NATO leaves Berlin
</H1>
The Russian military representative at NATO returned to Moscow for
personal reasons. The chairman of the NATO Military Committee, Naumann,
stated that General Savarsin (sp?) had not been ordered to return
because of protest against NATO air maneuvers, as was first reported.
The Russian general renewed his visa. It had been reported that Russia
had been annoyed with NATO air maneuvers over Macedonia and Albania.

<H1>
All first-generation ICE trains to be converted
</H1>
Barely 2 weeks after the train accident in Eschede with more than 100
deaths, the Federal Railways began the conversion of all firstgeneration ICE trains. Operation of 59 ICE trains with old tires was
prohibited by the Federal Railways Office in Bonn. A press release
stated that the prohibition will remain in effect until evidence of
an operational safety has been firmly established.
The railway accident in Eschede is said to have been caused by a
faulty tire.
Two of the 59 old ICE trains were back in service today with new
wheels.
According to the Federal Railways, the remaining 57 trains will be
equipped with brand new wheels or solid wheels like those found on
second-generation ICE trains. 120 replacement trains are currently in
use. Delays of up to 30 minutes can still be expected in the next few
days. It is not possible to predict how long the conversion will take.
Federal President Herzog will take part in Sunday=92s funeral ceremonies
for the victims of Eschede.

<H1>
Two people die in a collosion between train and passenger car
</H1>
A local train collided with a passenger train in Bad Muenster-Eifel.
Two people died, and two more received life-threatening injuries.
According to the Federal Railways, the train had collided with the car
at a railroad crossing without gates and dragged the car for
approximately 20 meters.

Deutsche Bank denies assisting customers in tax evasions
</H1>
The Deutsche Bank denied having given customers systematic assistance
in tax evasions. Based on suspicion, a large force of approximately
300 investigators searched the bank=92s central office in Frankfurt and
branch offices in several other cities. Employees of Deutsche Bank are
said to have assisted customers in avoiding the Treasury and investing
their money in Luxemburg and Switzerland.

<H1>
Baden-Wuerttemberg prohibits transports of nuclear waste
</H1>
Baden-Wuerttemberg will not allow any new transports of nuclear waste
from its 3 nuclear power plants until violations of exceeding
radioactive limits from mid-May are completely resolved. Environmental
Minister Schaufler said, following a meeting with the managers of the
nuclear power plants, that any future limit violations must be avoided
during the whole transport. His ministry had created a working team in
order to ensure that this demand be met.

<H1>
German soccer fans fight with French police
</H1>
An hour before the start of the World Cup game between Germany and the
United States, German fans fought with French police, throwing bottles
and rocks at the officers. Approximately 300 Germans attempted to break
through the barricades in front of the stadium.
